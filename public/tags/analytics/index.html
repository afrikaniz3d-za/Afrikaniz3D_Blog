Split-Push


 

I slowed down on the job searching last year after 100s of applications and seeing posts from creators like Jim Chuong. When you also have to deal with things like ghost job postings, and some instances of postings being for something else altogether, You really do need a different strategy when networking isn’t as easy and it’s an industry you’re fairly new in.

This is now the sixth year I’ve been a creator posting to social media. Although the bulk of that has been as a 3D designer, I’m slowly finding gaps for me to marry the two so that the codeswitch doesn’t feel as jarring. I alluded to this in the last blog and it turns out it’s related to Cognitive Load Theory with some Neuroplasticity. It really explains why I got so little else done on the days I set aside to work on data projects - the brain was tapped-out. A lot of it, I think, was also related to me still trying to wrap my head around the new problems coupled with starting with templates that were too complicated for someone unfamiliar.
